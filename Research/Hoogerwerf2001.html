Theory

This is my current research at FSU. This is the topic for my master thesis.

Abstract

We use Gaia DR3 astrometry data (proper motions and parallaxes) to trace back potential runaway stars from the neighbourhood of 399 young open clusters. Amongst these, x have atleast one potential runaway candidate. For each cluster, a Hurtzsprung-Russel Diagram is made to determine the membership to the cluster and also determine the temperature of the runaway. With this, we have x runaway candidates. Their properties are discussed in detail in the later sections. Some of these young clusters have known pulsars nearby, which is a strong indicator of a supernova event in the past. The supernova remanant is not known/recognised for these because these might be invisible (dissipated already). The age of the pulsar thus provides the upper limit of the dissipation time of the supernova remnant.
Observations of the spectra of the runaway candidates have been taken from the university observatoy of Jena to confirm/correct the temperature estimates of the runaways.
The aim is to provide further evidence for the Binary Supernova Ejection Scenario (BSS), adding to the past findings.
